OneShot (2016) &

World Machine Edition (2022)

OneShot is one of the few games that have originality to it. Due to how it interact
with the user's computer to solve some hidden puzzles (not in-game),
it's what makes the game so damn special. Created on RPG Maker XP in 2014,
and released fully in 2016 by Future Cat LLC & Degica, the game introduces to the player
the main protagonist of the game, Niko, a cat-like child that wakes up on a mysterious world
that him/herself does not recognize it. On the beginning of the game,
Niko encounters a computer, known as The Wolrd Machine, that interacts with the player.
Upon exploring, Niko learns that he/she's the "Messiah" (Saviour of the World),
and can speak towards it's God (The Player/Us). The player's mission is to guide Niko and
bring the "Sun" of the World (represented as a lightbulp) to it's central tower,
resided to the Refuge city, to restore the light of the world.
The player guides Niko on the three areas (Barrens, Glen and Refuge) and upon reaching the tower,
the Machine once again interacts with the player, revealing that they can either Bring the Sun
to the top of the tower (trapping Niko), or break the sun and return Niko to it's homeworld
(thus destroying this world). But can you do both of them? Can you get a Better Ending?
What's special about this game (like I said) is that, you need to do something that it's not in-game
to solve some puzzles, and also (since Niko and the World Machine can interact with the Player)
it's pretty obvious that the game BREAKS THE FORTH WALL, something that we don't see
that often on many videogames (like Max Payne, High on Life, The Stanley Parable, etc.).
OneShot: World Machine Edition was released for the Nintendo Switch in 2022.
Despite some cuts from the original game and added a PC-like UI for the puzzle parts,
it's still the same game, but played on another console!
